3 ways to spend 2 weeks in Iceland chasing the aurora

Aurora viewing is a probability game — dark skies, cloud gaps, and your chosen latitude all matter. These three shapes trade off how much time you spend driving versus settled, and how much you diversify across Iceland's weather regions.

The canonical Iceland trip: Route 1 in a loop, Reykjavík out through Snæfellsnes, up the north coast to Akureyri and Mývatn, down the east fjords, across the glacier south, back home. Every region's weather, every major landscape.

Week 1 · Heading north and east

Day 1–2
Reykjavík. Land, settle, pick up the rental car. Harpa, Hallgrímskirkja, a long dinner. Sky Lagoon if you land late. First aurora chance on night 2 if you drive 20 min out to Reykjanes.
Day 3
Drive to Snæfellsnes. 3h. Stop at Kirkjufell, sleep in Hellnar or Arnarstapi. Dark-sky night 1.
Day 4
Across to Akureyri. Long day (6–7h) via Borgarnes. Break in Siglufjörður if you have energy.
Day 5
Akureyri + Goðafoss. Town morning, waterfall afternoon, dinner back in town. Aurora viable just outside town.
Day 6–7
Mývatn + Diamond Circle. Pseudocraters, Dimmuborgir lava maze, Mývatn Nature Baths, Dettifoss, Ásbyrgi canyon. Húsavík whale watching day trip. Two dark-sky nights in the darkest part of Iceland.

Week 2 · Back through the south

Day 8–9
Eastfjords (Seyðisfjörður / Egilsstaðir). Rainbow street, coastal drive, remote geothermal springs (Vök Baths). Remote and dark.
Day 10
Höfn area. Vatnajökull views, langoustine dinner. Ice and sky together.
Day 11
Jökulsárlón + Diamond Beach → Vík. Iceberg lagoon, ice on black sand. Settle in Vík.
Day 12
Vík area. Reynisfjara basalt columns, Skógafoss + Seljalandsfoss, Sólheimajökull glacier walk option.
Day 13
Golden Circle, overnight near Selfoss. Þingvellir, Geysir, Gullfoss. Secret Lagoon.
Day 14
Reykjavík, depart.

South Focus, with a Snæfellsnes add

Three bases — Reykjavík, Snæfellsnes, Vík — covering the southwest peninsula, the Golden Circle, and the glacier-ice-and-waterfall south. Less driving, more settled nights. Weather delays are easier to absorb because you're never far from your base.

Week 1 · Reykjavík hub + west

Day 1–3
Reykjavík. Land, city walk, Blue Lagoon or Sky Lagoon, Reykjanes Peninsula day trip (Seltún geothermal, Gunnuhver, Kleifarvatn). Short drive out of town for aurora.
Day 4–6
Snæfellsnes. Kirkjufell, Arnarstapi coastal walk, Snæfellsjökull, Djúpalónssandur black pearl beach, Budir church. Three dark-sky nights; aim for clear ones.
Day 7
Drive to Vík. Via Seljalandsfoss + Skógafoss. Check in at Vík.

Week 2 · Glaciers + south + Golden Circle

Day 8
Jökulsárlón day. Iceberg lagoon, Diamond Beach, sea stacks.
Day 9
Reynisfjara + Skaftafell. Basalt cliffs, puffins in summer, glacier hike option (or ice cave in winter).
Day 10
Buffer day. Rest, hot tub, explore or save for weather. If you've had bad aurora nights, push farther inland tonight.
Day 11
Drive to Golden Circle area. Þingvellir, Geysir, Gullfoss.
Day 12–13
Reykjavík. Whale watching, museum day, cafes, last Icelandic meal.
Day 14
Depart.

Two-base aurora strategy: Reykjavík + Akureyri

Split the trip between the southwest and the north with a domestic flight instead of a long drive. Aurora-viewing odds go up because you have two independent weather regions — if the south is clouded in for a week, the north might not be.
